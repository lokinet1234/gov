Introduction

This case presents a clear pattern of malicious prosecution and abuse of process, stemming from the plaintiff's deliberate manipulation of legal proceedings.

The defendant's constitutional rights have been systematically violated through a calculated campaign of misrepresentation that perverts the justice system for personal gain.

Note: The term "Kafkaesque" refers to a situation marked by surreal complexity and bureaucratic irrationality.
01

Misconduct & Concealment of Evidence

A prosecutor's constitutional duty is to disclose all material exculpatory evidence. This is not merely a procedural formality but a cornerstone of a fair trial.

Core Principle: The Duty to Disclose

The prosecution's role is not simply to win but to see "that justice shall be done."

Brady v. Maryland (1963)
02

Malicious Prosecution

A claim for malicious prosecution arises when legal action is initiated for an improper purpose and without a reasonable basis.

"Half of the truth may amount to a lie if it is understood to be the whole."— Prosser & Keeton
03

Case Study: Feiner v. New York

A central issue is whether speech can be curtailed due to a hostile reaction from listeners. This case established the "heckler's veto" doctrine.

Majority Opinion

Feiner was arrested not for his speech, but for the dangerous reaction it engendered.

Dissenting Opinion

Police must first protect the speaker's right to talk, even from a hostile crowd.
